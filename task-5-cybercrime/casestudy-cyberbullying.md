🛡️ Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
In recent years, UPI fraud has become one of the most common cybercrimes in India.
This type of fraud typically occurs when scammers trick users into sharing sensitive information or approving fraudulent transactions.
A common scenario involves a fraudster calling or messaging the victim while pretending to be a bank official or a customer from an online marketplace.
The scammer claims that money will be credited to the victim’s account and sends a “collect request” via a UPI app.
The victim, unaware of the process, enters their UPI PIN, thinking they are receiving money. 
Instead, the amount gets deducted from their account.
In other cases, fake links or QR codes are shared, which redirect users to phishing pages or trigger unauthorized payments.
These scams mainly target people who are new to digital payments, elderly individuals, or those who lack awareness of how UPI works.
The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, data theft, and mental stress.
Victims often lose significant amounts of money and may find it difficult to recover their funds.
To prevent such fraud, users should never share their UPI PIN, avoid clicking on unknown links, and verify requests before approving payments. 
Awareness and caution are the best defenses against such cybercrimes.
